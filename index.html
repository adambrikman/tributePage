Dr. David J. Malan

The man who inspired me to become a programmer.

Facts about Dr. Malan:

  • 1999 - Earned a Bachelor of Arts in Computer Science (cum laude) and became the youngest instructor in Harvard Extension school's history (at the time).
  • 2003 - Became an Emergency Medical Technician (EMT) and provided emergency care at local events. Dr. Malan continued to provide these services on a volunteer basis for nine years, until 2012.
  • 2004 - Earned a Master of Science in Computer Science.
  • 2005 - Founded a startup called "Diskaster" which focused on recovering data from hard drives and memory cards.
  • 2007 - Present - Earned a Ph.D. in Computer Science and was named the Gordon McKay Professor of the Practice Computer Science. During this time, Dr. Malan taught several courses, including the introduction to computer science course, better known as "CS50". Under his supervision, CS50 became the largest course at Harvard University with 694 students and the largest MOOC (Massive Open Online Course) with over one-million registrants.
"Nothing is impossible once you have a keyboard at your disposal." -- Dr. David J. Malan
